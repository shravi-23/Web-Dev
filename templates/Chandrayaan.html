Chandrayaan Mission Overview

The Chandrayaan mission is part of India’s lunar exploration efforts led by ISRO. It started with the successful launch of Chandrayaan-1 in 2008, followed by Chandrayaan-2 in 2019, and the recent Chandrayaan-3 mission in 2023.

Chandrayaan-1: India's First Lunar Mission (2008)

Chandrayaan-1, launched in October 2008, was India’s first mission to the Moon. The mission aimed to survey the lunar surface, study its minerals, and detect the presence of water molecules. It was a major breakthrough as it discovered water on the Moon's surface, a landmark achievement for space exploration.

Chandrayaan-2: India's Ambitious Moon Mission (2019)

Launched in July 2019, Chandrayaan-2 included an orbiter, lander (Vikram), and rover (Pragyan). Although the lander did not achieve a soft landing, the orbiter continues to provide valuable data on the Moon’s surface, including high-resolution images and spectral data.

Chandrayaan-3: Successful Moon Landing (2023)

Chandrayaan-3 was launched in July 2023 and successfully achieved a soft landing on the Moon’s south pole, making India the fourth country to land a spacecraft on the Moon. The mission’s success is a major milestone in India's space exploration history.

The Chandrayaan missions continue to enhance India's capability in space exploration, and Chandrayaan-3's success paves the way for future lunar missions.
